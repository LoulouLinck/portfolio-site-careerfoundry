About me

Background & Achievements

As a Cultural Heritage scholar with a deep understanding of cultural differences, I offer a valuable asset in today's globalised world. My background includes debating moral and ethical issues inherent in Culture, allowing me to negotiate contested matters skillfully and develop innovative solutions.

In my professional journey, I prioritised people's stories and experiences. After gaining an in depth insight into the daily strategies of Japanese elderly to preserve local culture, my research strived to promote their role in cultural management and revitalization. As a Cultural Mediator for Stadtmuseum Berlin, I worked with diverse audiences collecting feedbacks through debates and continuous reporting. My work assisted their demand for better representations and accessibility to cultural infrastructures, in face of a conservative institution.


Transition

Not too many great things happened in 2020. Locked in my native countryside, I started coding with a bootcamp on Udemy. And I got hooked! Trained so far with qualitative data as a Humanities graduate, I reused the analytical and problem-solving parts of my brain, this time working with quantitative data.

I enjoyed the challenge of diving into this new world with the same enthusiasm I would studying different Cultures. There’s nothing quite like a blank page. The library rat in me was especially fond of organising consistent data, its infrastructure and protection. I am very grateful for this opportunity and look forward to some team action!


Goals

“The end or purpose of design is to improve or at least maintain the “habitability” of the world in all its dimensions” - Alain Findeli.

With a focus on people-centred cultural management as a scholar, at the heart of my discipline and ethos is the human and its wellbeing, not scientific discoveries or quantifiable outcomes. This naturally orientates my interest in designing philanthropic projects serving the user’s best interest in the Education, Culture, Environment and Social sectors.
